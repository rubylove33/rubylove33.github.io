Privacy Policy

Futureseed ("the App") values your privacy and complies with applicable data protection laws. This Policy explains what information may be processed through the App, why it is used, how it is retained or deleted, and what rights users have.

Article 1 — Information We Process

The App can be used without account registration. For service operation, the following information may be processed:

  1. User-entered data: goals, reflections/thought notes, keywords, action plans, and settings such as reminders and language.
  2. Device-stored data: local data required to provide app features.
  3. Cloud backup data (when backup feature is used): if the user initiates a backup, goal and record data is stored in the user's own cloud account (iOS: iCloud, Android: Google Drive App Data folder). This data is stored solely in the user's own account and is never accessed or retained by the developer.
  4. Payment-related data (for premium purchase): minimum status data required to confirm purchase entitlement.
  5. Advertising and stability analytics data (when enabled): non-identifying data such as Device ID, Advertising ID, product interaction, advertising data, and crash/performance data.

Article 2 — Purpose of Processing

The App processes information for the following purposes:

  1. Providing goal creation, tracking, and record features.
  2. Providing notification (reminder) features.
  3. Providing cloud backup and restore features (only when explicitly initiated by the user).
  4. Enabling premium features and reflecting purchase status.
  5. Serving ads (free version).
  6. Stability analysis and feature improvement.

Article 3 — Retention and Deletion

  1. App data (goals, records, settings) is stored locally on the user's device in principle.
  2. If the user uses the cloud backup feature, backup data is stored in the user's own cloud account (iCloud or Google Drive). That data is governed by the respective platform's policies, and the developer cannot access or delete it. To remove backup data, please do so directly through the platform's settings.
  3. If the user deletes/resets data in-app or uninstalls the App, locally stored data may be deleted immediately and may be difficult to recover. Cloud backup data, if any, may remain separately.
  4. The developer does not retain user-entered data on its own servers.

Article 4 — Third-Party Service Providers

The App may use third-party SDKs or platform services for ads, payments, cloud backup, and app stability/analytics.

  • Data that may be processed: Device ID, Advertising ID, product interaction, advertising data, crash/performance data.
  • Purpose: third-party advertising, analytics, app functionality improvement, and cloud backup.

Example services:

  • Google AdMob (ads)
  • Apple/Google billing platforms (in-app purchase)
  • Apple iCloud Key-Value Storage (iOS cloud backup — stored in user's own account)
  • Google Drive App Data folder (Android cloud backup — stored in user's own account, requires Google Sign-In)

Data handled by third-party services is governed by each provider's privacy policy.

  • Apple: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/
  • Google: https://policies.google.com/privacy

Article 5 — User Rights and Privacy Choices (GDPR and Applicable U.S. State Privacy Laws)

  1. Users in the European Economic Area (EEA), the UK, and Switzerland, where applicable, may have rights to access, correct, delete, restrict processing of, and withdraw consent for their personal data. The App does not store user-entered personal data on the developer's own servers. If the App is deleted or in-app data is erased, local device data may be deleted. Cloud backup data, if the user chose to create backups, may remain separately in the user's own iCloud or Google Drive account.
  2. The free version of the App may serve ads through Google AdMob. In connection with ad serving and measurement, information such as Advertising ID, Device ID, product interaction data, and similar identifiers may be processed by Google and parties involved in Google's ad-serving services, subject to applicable law and platform policy.
  3. Depending on the user's region, consent choices, platform permissions, and ad availability, the App may serve personalized ads, non-personalized ads, limited ads, or no ads for a period of time. Where required by law, the App may present an in-app privacy or consent message before requesting ads.
  4. On iOS, the App may request App Tracking Transparency (ATT) permission. ATT is a platform-level permission and is separate from privacy choices that may be required under GDPR or applicable U.S. state privacy laws. Users can change ATT choices in iOS settings. On Android, users can manage ad-related preferences through device or Google settings. Example paths include:
    • iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking
    • Android: Settings → Google → Ads
  5. Where available, users can revisit advertising-related privacy choices through the App's in-app "Ad Privacy Choices" entry point. This in-app choice flow is distinct from the operating system's ATT setting.
  6. Users covered by CCPA/CPRA and certain other applicable U.S. state privacy laws may have rights to opt out of the "sale," "sharing," or use of personal information for targeted advertising, as defined by applicable law. The App does not sell personal information for monetary consideration. However, identifiers such as advertising identifiers may be processed, disclosed, or shared as part of Google AdMob ad serving, personalized advertising, non-personalized advertising, limited ads, or ad measurement, to the extent permitted by applicable law.
  7. If required consent choices are not yet completed, or if a legally permitted ad serving mode is not available for the request, some ad requests may be delayed or unavailable until the required privacy flow has been completed.

Article 6 — Third-Party Disclosure and Sharing

Except for the third-party services (SDKs/platforms) stated in this Policy, the App does not provide or outsource personal information to third parties without user consent. Cloud backup data is stored in the user's own Apple or Google account, not on the developer's servers.

Article 7 — Policy Updates

This Policy may be amended due to legal, service, or SDK changes. Material changes will be notified through in-app notice or app updates.
